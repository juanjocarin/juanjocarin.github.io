description: 'The population of laptops follows a binomial distribution with probability \(p\) of being a Mac. Hence, in a sample of \(N\) laptops the number of Macs would be \(N_1 = p \cdot N\). The sample mean and variance are $$\bar{x} = \frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^N x_i = \frac{N_1}{N} = p$$ $$s_x = \frac{1}{N-1}\sum_{i=1}^N \left(x_i - \bar{x}\right)^2 = \frac{N_1(N-N_1)}{N(N-1)} = \frac{N}{N-1} p (1-p)$$Our hypothesis is that Macs are more common:$$H_0: \bar{X} = 0.5; H_1: \bar{X} > 0.5$$To be able to reject the null hypothesis at the \(5\%\) confidence level, the following inequalities (where \(t = t_{0.95, N-1}\)) should hold:$$\begin{aligned}\frac{\bar{x} - 0.5}{s_x/\sqrt{N}} \geq t &\Rightarrow \frac{p-\frac{1}{2}}{\sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{N-1}}} \geq t \\ &\Rightarrow \frac{2p-1}{2} \geq t\sqrt{\frac{p(1-p)}{N-1}} \\ &\Rightarrow (2p-1)^2(N-1) \geq 4t^2p(1-p) \\ &\Rightarrow 4p^2(N+t^2-1)-4p(N+t^2-1)+(N-1) \geq 0 \end{aligned}$$For simplicity, let''s denote \(N+t^2-1\) by \(\theta\):$$\begin{aligned} 4\theta p^2-4\theta p + N-1 \geq 0 \Rightarrow p \geq\frac{4\theta+\sqrt{16\theta^2-16\theta(N-1)}}{8\theta} &= \frac{1+\sqrt{1-\frac{N-1}{\theta}}}{2} \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\left(1+\sqrt{\frac{\theta-N-1}{\theta}}\right) \\ &= \frac{1}{2}\left(1+\frac{t}{\sqrt{\theta}}\right)\end{aligned}$$'
